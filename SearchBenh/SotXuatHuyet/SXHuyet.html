What is Dengue ?

Dengue It is an infectious disease caused by a virus Dengue , the disease is transmitted to humans by the Aedes aegypti mosquito, also known as the midges. The disease can progress to dangerous complications and even death. At present, dengue fever is a disease that has no vaccine or specific treatment.

DenGue it is an infectious disease caused by a virus Dengue . Virus Dengue has 4 serotypes namely DEN-1, DEN-2, DEN-3 and DEN-4. The virus is transmitted from an infected person to a healthy person by mosquito bites. The Aedes aegypti mosquito is the main vector of the disease. Character of DenGue is fever, hemorrhage and plasma leakage, which can lead to hypovolemic shock, coagulopathy, organ failure, if not diagnosed early and treated promptly, can lead to death.

Therefore Dengue is the most common mosquito-borne viral disease in humans. In recent years the disease has become a major international public health concern. Worldwide, approximately 2.5 billion people live in endemic areas. The geographical spread of both vectors (mosquitoes) and viruses has led to an increase in morbidity over the past 25 years as well as the possibility of outbreaks of various serotypes in urban areas in the tropics.

1. Where does the disease occur??

Dengue is mainly transmitted by the mosquito Aedes aegypti and is usually distributed in tropical countries. Disease Dengue can happen at any time. However, high humidity and temperature are favorable conditions for the reproduction and development of mosquitoes, increasing the possibility of disease transmission. In Vietnam, the disease occurs all year round, often increasing in the rainy season. The disease occurs in both children and adults.

The incidence of the disease worldwide has increased dramatically in recent years. The disease is now endemic in more than 100 countries in Africa, the Americas, the Eastern Mediterranean region, Southeast Asia and the Western Pacific. Southeast Asia and the Western Pacific are the hardest hit regions. Before 1970, there were only 9 countries with endemic disease. This number more than quadrupled in 1995. The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that 50 to 100 million people are infected each year. 2. CAUSE OF DENGUE.

People infected with the virus DenGue bitten by a female mosquito of the genus Aedes. The Aedes aegypti mosquito is the predominant vector in most endemic areas. The Aedes aegypti mosquito is active during the day and only female mosquitoes bite and transmit disease. When a female Aedes mosquito sucks the blood of a patient infected with dengue virus, the virus will incubate in the mosquito's body for about 8 to 11 days. For the rest of their life after that, mosquitoes are at risk of transmitting disease to humans. When viruses enter the human body, they circulate in the blood for 2 to 7 days. During this time, if the Aedes mosquito sucks blood, the virus is transmitted to the mosquito.

3. How dangerous of Dengue?

Nowadays, Dengue do not have specific treatment and no vaccine to prevent the disease. The disease often causes large epidemics with many people infected at the same time, making treatment extremely difficult, can cause death, especially for children, causing great economic and social losses. More than 85% of dengue hemorrhagic fever cases and 90% of deaths occur in the southern provinces of Vietnam. In which, 90% of deaths from dengue fever are under 15 years old. Disease dengue because of virus DenGue caused with 4 types, denoted D1, D2, D3, D4. All four types of these viruses are capable of causing disease in Vietnam and alternately causing epidemics. Immunity formed after infection is only specific to each individual type, so it is understandable that a person can get dengue 4 times in his life with 4 different viruses.


4. Guidelines for disease prevention.
  • Avoid mosquito bites: Sleep under a mosquito net, do not let children play in a dark place, apply mosquito repellent.
  • Kill mosquitoes and swarms: the house is always cool, clean and tidy. Do not leave water containers or if any, cover them and regularly change and remove stagnant water.
  • In addition to the above instructions, please note that you should always closely monitor your body temperature, immediately notify your doctor if you notice signs of fever, absolutely do not arbitrarily take medicine without a doctor's prescription.

1. Classification of degrees of dengue fever.
Dengue divided into 3 levels (according to the World Health Organization - WHO in 2009):
  1. Dengue.
  2. Dengue hemorrhagic fever has warning signs.
  3. Severe Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (Dengue Hemorrhagic Shock).

Dengue has symptoms vary widely, ranging from mild to severe. The disease usually has a sudden onset and progresses through three stages: the febrile phase, the critical phase, and the convalescent phase. It should be noted that the fever stage corresponds to the normal form of dengue hemorrhagic fever, if it has passed the dangerous stage, it has changed to the level of dengue with warning signs and severe dengue. . At that time, it is necessary to send the patient to the hospital immediately because it can lead to dangerous complications. Early detection of the disease and understanding the clinical problems in each stage of the disease will help early diagnosis, correct and timely treatment, in order to save the patient's life.

Fever phase.

The febrile phase will appear after the incubation period, which lasts 4 to 10 days after being bitten by an infected mosquito.

  • The patient showed signs of sudden high fever, continuously 39-40 degrees Celsius, lasting 2-7 days, difficult to reduce fever.
  • Severe headache in the forehead area, pain in the two orbits behind the eyeballs.
  • There may be rash, rash, skin congestion.
  • Loss of appetite, nausea.
  • Muscle pain, joint pain.
  • Positive ligature test.

High fever that is difficult to bring down is a typical symptom of dengue fever

Dangerous period.

Usually on the 3rd to 7th day of illness, the patient may still have a fever or have decreased fever. A drop in temperature does not necessarily mean that the patient is recovering, on the contrary, special attention should be paid to the presentation of dengue hemorrhagic fever with warning signs and progression to hemorrhagic fever. Dangerous Dengue .

  • Plasma leakage due to increased vascular permeability (usually lasts 24 - 48 hours); pleural effusion, interstitial tissue, peritoneum, eyelid swelling, hepatomegaly, may be painful. If there is a lot of plasma leakage, it will lead to shock with signs of palpitations, restlessness or lethargy, cold extremities, cold and moist skin, small rapid pulse, stuck blood pressure (maximum and minimum blood pressure difference ≤ 25 mmHg. ), low blood pressure or unmeasured blood pressure, little urine.
  • Hemorrhage: Subcutaneous haemorrhage, sporadic purpura or petechiae, usually on the front of the legs and on the insides of the arms, abdomen, thighs, ribs, or bruises. Hemorrhage in the mucosa: Bleeding from the nose, gums, blood in the urine. Menstruation lasts or occurs earlier than the period. Visceral bleeding such as: gastrointestinal, lung, brain are severe manifestations (vomiting/vomiting blood, black stools due to internal bleeding).
  • In some cases of severe dengue fever, there may be signs of organ failure such as severe hepatitis, encephalitis, myocarditis. These severe manifestations may occur in patients with no obvious signs of plasma leakage or in shock.
  • Abdominal pain, nausea, cold hands and feet, panicking (this is a shock syndrome caused by internal bleeding causing blood loss and lowering blood pressure), requiring immediate emergency care.
Recovery phase.

About 24-48 hours after the dangerous phase, the patient's body gradually reabsorbs fluid from the interstitial tissue into the vessel lumen, this phase will last for about 48-72 hours.

  • The patient's fever was gone, his general condition improved, his appetite returned, hemodynamic stability was stable, and he urinated a lot.
  • There may be bradycardia and ECG changes.
  • During this period, excessive fluid administration to the patient can cause pulmonary edema or heart failure.
