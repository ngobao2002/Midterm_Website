AXIT-FOLIC là gì ?

FOLIC ACID or Vitamin B9 or Folacin or Folate are water-soluble forms of vitamin B9, necessary and important in the daily nutrition of the human body, especially in pregnant women and infants to serve. cell renewal processes that help cells grow and develop.
FOLIC ACID where is it?
FOLIC ACID naturally present in food and can also be obtained from dietary supplements. Foods that are good sources of folic acid include:

  • Vegetables like spinach, lettuce, okra, asparagus, turnips, broccoli, brussels sprouts and spinach, and nuts like dried beans, peas or yeast, and mushrooms.
  • Fruits such as bananas, melons, lemons, orange juice, and grapefruit.
  • Beef liver and kidney.
  • In addition, manufacturers often add folic acid to foods such as milk, cereals, flour, bread, pasta, baking ingredients, cookies, and cookies.

Uses of FOLIC ACID

Folic acid helps the body build and sustain new cells while also preventing DNA alterations that can lead to cancer. Folic acid is a medication used to treat folic acid insufficiency and some kinds of anemia (low red blood cell count) caused by folic acid deficiency. To treat pernicious anemia, folic acid is sometimes used with additional drugs. The medicine, however, cannot cure vitamin B12 deficiency and cannot prevent spinal cord injury.

The role of folic acid in pregnancy

  • Reduces risk of cancer: Folic acid may reduce the risk of cancer-related diseases by a small percentage, such as breast cancer. Some people use folic acid to prevent colon or cervical cancer. It is used to prevent heart disease and stroke as well as to reduce the level of a chemical in the blood (hemolysis). However, these hypotheses are facing a lot of debate and cannot come to an exact conclusion.
  • Prevent some other diseases: Folic acid is also used for dementia, dementia, age-related hearing loss, reducing signs of aging, weak bones (osteoporosis), restless legs, trouble sleeping, depression, neuralgia, myalgia, AIDS, vitiligo and Fragile-X syndrome.
  • Prevention of anemia: Folic acid plays a very important role in the supply of blood cells to the body, they help create new cells, including red blood cells. pregnant women and fetuses to prevent anemia, miscarriages, premature births, susceptibility to postpartum mental disorders, fetal malnutrition or heart disease in newborns vascular, cleft palate if the mother is severely deficient in folic acid.
  • Prevent birth defects: During pregnancy, the baby's brain and spinal cord have been formed in the uterus, so supplementing with enough folic acid at this important time will help the fetus develop normally and be healthy. It helps prevent serious birth defects in the brain and spinal cord such as neural tube defects (NTDs), spina bifida, or possibly a partial brain defect.

DEMAND

Folic acid is available in the following forms and strengths:

  • Capsules, oral drugs: 5 mg, 20 mg.
  • Solution, injection, such as sodium folate: 5 mg/mL.
  • Tablets and pills: 400 mcg, 800 mcg, 1 mg.
  • Tablets, oral [preservative-free]: 400 mcg, 800 mcg.

For pregnant women:

  • Women who are planning to become pregnant: 400 mg/ngay.
  • Pregnant: 600 mg/ngay.
  • Breastfeeding women: 500 mg/ngay.

AXIT-FOLIC What disease can it cause?

Missing ACID-FOLIC:
  • Depression, difficulty concentrating, forgetfulness, irritability, memory loss.
  • Nausea, abdominal pain and diarrhea, loss of appetite persists.
  • Body ache.
  • Pale skin.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Scurvy disease.
  • Mouth sores, swollen tongue.
  • Decreased taste.
  • Atherosclerosis.
  • Alzheimer.
Excess ACID-FOLIC:
  • High levels of folic acid can mask a vitamin B12 deficiency.
  • Accelerates the aging process of nerve cells.
  • Inhibits brain development in children.
  • Increases the likelihood of tumor growth.

ISSUES NEEDED NOTES

When using folic acid vitamins need to be careful

  • Folic acid should be taken between meals.
  • Vitamin C will increase iron absorption. Therefore, you should take iron - folic acid tablets with orange juice or fruit juice.
  • Avoid taking the drug with tea, coffee, and alcohol because folic acid will reduce absorption.
  • Taking folic acid often causes constipation, so drink lots of water and eat a lot of fiber.
  • Folic acid should be taken exactly as prescribed by your doctor. Do not take in larger amounts or for longer than prescribed. Follow the directions on the medicine label. Take folic acid with plenty of water. Your doctor may sometimes change your dose to make sure the medicine works best for you. Tell your doctor if your condition does not improve or gets worse.
