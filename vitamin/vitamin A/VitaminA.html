What is VITAMIN-A?

VITAMIN-A It is an essential nutrient for humans. It does not exist as a single compound, but in several forms. Vitamin A is found in many foods, it exists in foods of animal origin as retinol, and in plants as carotene (provitamin A). Liver, egg yolk, butter, milk, cheese, water spinach, water spinach, collard greens, pumpkin, carrots, mangoes contain a lot of vitamin A.

Uses of VITAMIN-A

VITAMIN-A plays an important role in the growth process, helping children develop normally.

  • Vitamin-A is involved in eye function in low light conditions. Its manifestation is known as "night blindness", which is an early sign of vitamin A deficiency.
  • Vitamin-A is involved in the visual function of the eye, which is the ability to see in low light conditions. When vitamin A deficiency occurs, the eye's ability to see in low light will be reduced, this phenomenon often appears at dusk, so it is called "night blindness". Night blindness is an early clinical manifestation of vitamin A deficiency.
  • Vitamin-A participates in the body's immune response processes, increasing the body's resistance to diseases. If vitamin A deficiency, children are susceptible to infections and when infected, the disease lasts longer, the risk of death is higher.
  • Vitamin-A supports immune function by supporting the development and distribution of T cells, a type of white blood cell that protects your body from infection. What's more, vitamin A supports healthy skin cells, reproduction, and fetal development.

VITAMIN-A What disease can it cause?

Short VITAMIN-A:
  • Night blindness: reduced vision in low light conditions.
  • Dry and burning sensation in the eyes.
  • Eye pain ranges from mild to severe.
  • Red eyes.
  • Upper and lower eyelids stick together.
  • Temporary or permanent blurred vision.
  • Fear of strong light.
  • Or eye pain.
Thừa VITAMIN-A:
  • Symptoms of liver toxicity.
  • Bone changes, joint pain.
  • Nails break easily.
  • Change in vision, papilledema.
  • Osteoarthritis disease.
  • Headache, dizziness, trouble sleeping.
  • Loss of concentration, irritability.
  • Nausea, vomiting, fatigue.
  • Decreased appetite and difficulty gaining weight.

NHỮNG VẤN ĐỀ CẦN LƯU Ý

1. Consequences of excess VITAMIN-A

  • Excess vitamin A -> causes poisoning.
  • Consuming large amounts of vitamin A -> liver toxicity, joint pain, headache, bone changes, vomiting, dry, flaky skin or bulging fontanelles in children.
  • Pregnant women using vitamin A in excess of 3,000mcg daily -> cause teratogenicity.
2. Improve meals, nutrition education and communication
Ensure adequate intake of nutrients and vitamin A. Breastfeeding is done because breast milk is the best source of vitamin A for young children.
Your child's complementary diet should include foods rich in vitamin A, such as:
  • Food of animal origin: Eggs, fish, meat, liver, oval, shrimp...
  • Plant-based foods: In our country, vegetables with notable beta-carotene content are water spinach, lettuce, water spinach, lettuce, amaranth, green onions, shallots, herbs, assorted. fruits such as gac, carrots, ripe fruits such as papaya, mango...
  • In addition, complementary foods for children need oil or fat to enhance absorption of vitamin A and vitamin D. Strengthen mass media education to improve people's knowledge on how to prevent vitamin A deficiency, guide Practical guide for families to know how to choose and use foods rich in vitamin A for family meals and health care.
3. Supplement capsulesVITAMIN-A high dose
  • High-dose vitamin A supplementation is made for high-risk groups who receive vitamin A every year, usually every 6 months.
  • Subjects supplemented with high-dose vitamin A capsules: vitamin A supplements every 6 months for children 6-36 months old (can be extended to 60 months old in difficult areas with high malnutrition rate) and mothers within one month of giving birth.
  • In March 2011, the World Health Organization recommended stopping high-dose vitamin A supplementation for mothers after giving birth because many studies did not find it effective, however, Vietnam is discussing and not applying this recommendation.
4. EnhancementVITAMIN-A in some foods
Fortifying vitamin A into food is considered a long-term solution to prevent micronutrients in general and prevent vitamin A deficiency in particular. This solution has been successful in many countries: such as adding vitamin A to sugar (Central American countries), cooking oil (Philippines, Indonesia), instant noodles, fast food (Thailand).
There have been studies in our country that have tested vitamin A fortification in sugar, flour, and supplementary food (nutrition powder) for children, proving to improve the vitamin A status of users.
